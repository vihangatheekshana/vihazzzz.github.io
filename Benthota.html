BENTHOTA

Introduction to Turtle Beach in Benthota

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery aims to protect and conserve sea turtles by providing a safe and controlled environment for their nesting and hatching activities. The project works towards increasing turtle populations, educating the public about conservation, and raising awareness about the importance of preserving these magnificent creatures. Conservation Efforts: The hatchery collects sea turtle eggs that are vulnerable to predation or human interference from local beaches. These eggs are then carefully incubated in a controlled environment, replicating the natural nesting conditions. Once the hatchlings emerge, they are given proper care and nurturing until they are ready to be released into the ocean. Educational Programs: The Bentota Turtle Hatchery conducts educational programs and guided tours to raise awareness among visitors about sea turtle conservation. Visitors can learn about the different species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka, their life cycle, threats they face, and the importance of protecting their nesting habitats. Release of Hatchlings: One of the highlights of the hatchery is the opportunity to witness the release of the hatchlings into the sea. This allows visitors to actively participate in the conservation efforts and witness the incredible journey of the young turtles as they begin their lives in the ocean. Turtle Rehabilitation: In addition to conserving and releasing hatchlings, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery also provides a sanctuary for injured or sick turtles. These turtles receive medical treatment and rehabilitation to help them recover and, if possible, be released back into the wild. Community Involvement: The hatchery actively involves the local community in its conservation efforts. By engaging fishermen and residents, they promote responsible fishing practices and raise awareness about the importance of protecting sea turtles and their habitats. Tourism Impact: The Bentota Turtle Hatchery has become a popular tourist attraction, drawing visitors from around the world. The income generated from tourist visits and donations plays a crucial role in sustaining the hatchery's operations and supporting ongoing conservation efforts. By combining conservation efforts, education, and community involvement, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery contributes significantly to the preservation of sea turtles in Sri Lanka. It serves as a model for sustainable turtle conservation practices and inspires individuals to take action to protect these magnificent creatures and their fragile ecosystems.





Getting to know wild turtles in Benthota


Bentota, a coastal paradise in Sri Lanka, offers a unique opportunity to get up close and personal with wild turtles. The pristine beaches of Bentota serve as nesting grounds for various turtle species, making it a prime location for turtle enthusiasts and nature lovers. By exploring the beaches and observing these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat, visitors can develop a deeper understanding of the lives and behaviors of wild turtles. Embark on an unforgettable journey and discover the wonders of wild turtles in Bentota.
