Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery


Nestled on the enchanting shores of Sri Lanka, Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery stands as a vital sanctuary for the country's mesmerizing marine life. Let us embark on a journey to explore this remarkable conservation haven.

A Haven for Endangered Turtles

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery plays a pivotal role in preserving and protecting Sri Lanka's endangered turtle species. With a commitment to conservation and education, this sanctuary provides a safe environment for nesting and hatching, ensuring the survival of these magnificent creatures.


Turtle Species Found at the Hatchery

The hatchery is home to various turtle species, including the Green Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. Visitors can witness these incredible creatures up close, fostering a deeper appreciation for their conservation needs.


The Life Cycle of Turtles

At Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, visitors can witness the captivating life cycle of turtles. From observing nesting mothers laying their eggs to witnessing the emergence of tiny hatchlings, this sanctuary offers a unique opportunity to learn about the remarkable journey of these ancient reptiles.


Conservation Efforts and Research

The dedicated team at Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is engaged in extensive research and conservation efforts. Through monitoring and protection initiatives, they strive to increase the survival rates of turtle hatchlings and contribute to scientific knowledge.


Volunteering and Educational Programs

The hatchery provides a range of volunteering and educational programs for both locals and tourists. Participants can actively contribute to conservation efforts, learn about turtle biology, and raise awareness about the importance of protecting these species.


Releasing Hatchlings Into the Ocean

One of the most awe-inspiring experiences at the hatchery is witnessing the release of hatchlings into the ocean. Visitors can join in this unforgettable event, symbolizing hope and the continuation of these endangered turtle populations.


Ecotourism and Sustainable Practices

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery embraces sustainable practices and promotes responsible tourism. By generating awareness and income through ecotourism, the hatchery ensures the continued preservation of these fragile ecosystems and the welfare of the turtles that call it home.

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery stands as a shining example of conservation and education, protecting Sri Lanka's precious marine life for generations to come. So, plan your visit to Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery and embark on a remarkable journey that will leave you with a deep appreciation for the wonders of marine life and a lasting commitment to conservation.
