Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

Located in the idyllic coastal town of Kosgoda, Sri Lanka, the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery stands as a beacon of hope for endangered sea turtles and a testament to the dedication of conservationists. Established with the aim of protecting and rehabilitating these magnificent creatures, the hatchery has become a vital institution in the battle against the decline of turtle populations. The hatchery serves as a sanctuary for several species of sea turtles, including the Olive Ridley, Loggerhead, Green, Hawksbill, and Leatherback turtles. These species face numerous threats in their natural habitats, such as habitat destruction, pollution, and poaching. At Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, their mission is to provide a safe space for these turtles to lay their eggs and ensure the survival of the next generation. The process at the hatchery begins with the collection of turtle eggs from vulnerable nesting sites along the coast. Dedicated conservationists carefully relocate these eggs to protected hatcheries within the facility. This safeguards them from predators and human interference, significantly increasing the chances of successful hatching. Once the eggs hatch, the baby turtles are carefully nurtured until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. Education plays a crucial role at the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery. Visitors are provided with informative tours, where they learn about the life cycle of turtles, the threats they face, and the efforts being made to protect them. This awareness helps to foster a sense of responsibility and encourages individuals to take action to safeguard these remarkable creatures and their fragile ecosystems. Furthermore, the hatchery actively participates in conservation research and collaborates with local and international organizations to promote marine conservation efforts. They conduct studies on turtle behavior, nesting patterns, and the impact of environmental factors. These findings contribute to a better understanding of these species and inform conservation strategies on a broader scale. In addition to their conservation efforts, the hatchery also focuses on the rehabilitation of injured or sick turtles. With the help of veterinary experts, injured turtles are treated and nursed back to health. Once they have recovered, they are released back into the wild, giving them a second chance at life. The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery serves as a shining example of the positive impact that dedicated individuals can have on the preservation of endangered species. Through their tireless efforts, they not only protect turtles but also raise awareness about the importance of conservation among locals and visitors alike. By supporting the hatchery, people contribute to the ongoing conservation of these remarkable creatures and help ensure a sustainable future for marine life. Useful Information



If you are planning a getaway in the Kalutara area, go the extra mile and make a visit to the Kosgoda Turtle Conservation Project, to learn about these gentle and graceful marine creatures. The Turtle conservatory is open daily till 5.00 p.m. in the evening and entrance fee comes up to LKR.500. Upon arriving, visitors can spend around 30 minutes to 1 hour wandering about learning more about turtles such as their life cycles and hatching process.
